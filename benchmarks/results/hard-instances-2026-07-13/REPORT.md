# Hard-instance implementation report — 2026-07-13

## Outcome

The plan is implemented through Workstreams A, B, C, and E. Workstream D was
correctly skipped because its entry gate did not pass. Production defaults keep
materialized search-time reduction and cutset conditioning disabled.

On the complete 258-instance frozen MQT2040 corpus, AUTO now solves 194 cases,
up from 182. All twelve failures that still reproduced at the final 12 GiB
limit were count-vector allocation failures; the new scalar-result preflight
eliminates all twelve without introducing a timeout, refusal, or OOM.

| Frozen AUTO metric | Untouched baseline | Final |
|---|---:|---:|
| Solved | 182 | 194 |
| `max_fallback_vars` | 61 + 3 coarsely classified | 61 |
| `no_delegate` | included in the 64 above | 3 |
| OOM at 12 GiB | 12 | 0 |
| Solved-case wall time | 473.527 s | 149.917 s |
| p50 / p95 solved wall time | 0.001 / 15.868 s | 0.001 / 4.485 s |
| Maximum solved wall time | 75.452 s | 21.529 s |

The baseline predates the stable final-record taxonomy and labels all 64 policy
failures as `max_fallback_vars`; rerunning them with the final JSONL schema
separates the expected 61/3 split.

Every success and failure now ends with a `sop_solve_run_stats_v1` JSONL record.
The record carries a stable status/reason enum, partial counters, failure
residual sizes, cutset state, treewidth accounting, and the supplemental
diagnostic. Both benchmark runners consume that final record on nonzero exit.

## Workstream B — materialized reduction gate

The simplifier now reports degree-0/1/2 elimination counts and an exact zero
witness. Branch search can opt into rebuilding a dense active QSOP containing
the residual constant and artificial `norm_h`, simplifying it, and carrying
the resulting power-of-two amplitude exponent into a fresh residual.

The diagnostic covered all 64 policy refusals, eight candidates and both values
per case: 1,024 conditioning records plus 64 final summaries.

| One-level gate | Cases (of 64) |
|---|---:|
| At least one degree-2 merge | 64 |
| A candidate whose worse child has extra reduction | 64 |
| A child split into multiple components | 64 |
| Exact-zero child | 0 |
| Child already at the 64-variable fallback | 0 |

This decisively passed the Workstream B gate and justified the bounded cutset
pilot. Random small instances (`n <= 10`, `r ∈ {2,4,8,16}`) are differential
tested against exhaustive amplitudes, including odd/even target behavior,
doubling exponents, zero witnesses, and parent restoration.

## Workstream C — four-way ablation and default decision

| Configuration | Refusals solved | New timeout | Refusal-set wall | 24 controls solved | Control total | Control p95 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Baseline | 0 | 0 | 16.575 s | 24 | 7.786 s | 2.229 s |
| Materialized only | 0 | 0 | 15.449 s | 24 | 7.614 s | 2.224 s |
| Cutset only | 1 | 0 | 149.991 s | 24 | 7.672 s | 2.266 s |
| Cutset + materialized | 1 | 1 | 225.367 s | 24 | 7.692 s | 2.230 s |

Both cutset configurations solve QNN-25 (82.551 s without materialized
reduction, 45.236 s with it). The combined run times out on QNN-26. The solved
count gain is one, below the preferred five-case enablement threshold, while a
new timeout appears. Therefore cutset depth remains zero and materialized
reduction remains off by default.

The combined QNN-25 solve spent 6,484,701 ns in materialized reduction, far
below 20% of 45.236 seconds. Workstream D's two entry conditions therefore do
not both hold; rollback edge rewiring was not implemented.

## Workstream E — treewidth buckets and qwalk

Pre-bucket profiling justified the rewrite: factor-list discovery performed
518,077,903 scope tests on qwalk-10 and 2,110,468,300 on qwalk-11, consuming
1.996 s and 8.518 s respectively. The f64 path now uses largest-scope
elimination buckets, in-place last-bit sum-out with preserved allocation arity,
and a 512 MiB retained-pool cap (individual retention capped at 256 MiB).

| qwalk | Before wall | After wall | Scope tests before → after | Discovery before → after | Peak bytes before → after |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 10 | 5.312 s | 3.459 s | 518,077,903 → 0 | 1.996 s → 0.095 s | 106,892,224 → 79,563,840 |
| 11 | 35.077 s | 26.322 s | 2,110,468,300 → 0 | 8.518 s → 0.275 s | 403,178,656 → 271,582,240 |
| 12 | timeout | timeout | — | — | — |
| 13 | timeout | timeout | — | — | — |

The required alternative success condition is met: a focused qwalk-11 trace
attributes about 25.75 of 26.43 seconds to min-fill order generation, while
factor discovery, joins, and sum-out account for well under one second. The
remaining qwalk-12/13 bottleneck is therefore decomposition generation, not the
rewritten factor engine. Random-family size-24 solves fall from roughly 75 s to
about 21.5 s, with no regression in the 24 solved controls.
